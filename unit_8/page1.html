Sorry, it's still all just advertising

One of the funniest things I've witnessed over the past couple years in and around the ad agency business is the constant reductio ad absurdum that's been going on around the actual word 'advertising' itself.

It reminds me of this diet I once got into based around the theory that drinks were less fattening than solids.

I was at a groovy party regaling a rather charming model with the joys of drinking myself fit. She quickly put me in my place.

“Listen dummy,” she said, “if it isn't water it's food.”

It reminded me of something Fairfax Cone, founder of the original Foote, Cone, Belding (FCB), said. I'm presuming it was in answer to some asinine nonsense in a similarly delusional vein to mine above. It blows away every bit as instantly all the current self-serving, tortuous, cod-revolutionary faddism in marketing services that has come in on the digital tide:

“Advertising is what you do when you can't go see someone. That's all it is.”
